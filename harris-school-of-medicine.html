About the Project

A 360 educational showcase of contemporary medical instruction spaces, collaboration zones, and clinical learning environments.

Opportunity Gift Level Description
Clinical Simulation Lab $1 Million Naming Opportunity The Clinical Simulation Lab is where students and residents safely bridge the gap between classroom learning and real-world patient care. In this highly realistic, technology-enabled environment, learners can practice procedures, refine their judgment, and repeat complex scenarios until they achieve confidence and competence, all without putting patients at risk.

Simulation-based training has been shown to enhance clinical skills, improve decision-making under pressure, and reduce medical errors, making it an essential component of modern medical education. A named Clinical Simulation Lab will allow the School of Medicine to expand scenario-based learning, support interprofessional training, and prepare future physicians to deliver safer, more effective care from day one.
Anatomy and Skills Suite $500,000 Naming Opportunity The Anatomy and Skills Suite is the foundation on which every physician builds a lifetime of clinical practice. Here, learners gain a deep, three-dimensional understanding of the human body and develop the fine motor skills, precision, and respect that define excellent clinicians.

Through hands-on anatomy work, procedural skills training, and guided practice with expert faculty, students learn to connect what they see in textbooks to what they will encounter in the operating room, clinic, and emergency department. A named Anatomy and Skills Suite will ensure that future physicians are grounded in strong scientific knowledge, technical proficiency, and the professionalism that begins in the lab and carries forward to every patient encounter.
Multidisciplinary Team Rooms $250,000 Naming Opportunity Modern healthcare is delivered by teams, not individuals. The Multidisciplinary Team Rooms are dedicated spaces where students from medicine, nursing, pharmacy, and other health professions learn with, from, and about one another as they work through realistic clinical cases.

These rooms are designed to foster effective communication, role clarity, and collaborative decision-making, the core skills of high-functioning healthcare teams that improve safety, coordination, and patient experience. A named Multidisciplinary Team Room suite will help the School of Medicine graduate clinicians who are ready to lead and contribute within complex care teams from their very first day in practice.
Telemedicine Learning Hub $100,000 Naming Opportunity The Telemedicine Learning Hub is where future physicians and allied health professionals master the skills needed to deliver high-quality care in a digital, connected world. As virtual visits, remote monitoring, and digital health tools become integral to care, learners must be prepared not only to use technology, but to communicate, examine, and make decisions effectively at a distance.

This Hub will provide a dedicated environment for students to develop telemedicine competencies, technical fluency, digital professionalism, remote physical examination techniques, and equitable virtual care practices, within realistic, supervised scenarios. A named Telemedicine Learning Hub will position the School of Medicine at the forefront of digital health education and ensure that graduates are ready to care for patients wherever they are.
