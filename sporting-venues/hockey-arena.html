Why the Hockey Arena Matters

Ice hockey is more than a sport — it is a cultural institution that brings communities together across generations. The Hockey Arena is designed to serve as a home for professional and semi-professional teams while simultaneously providing accessible ice time for youth leagues, figure skating, recreational hockey, and public skating.

The facility features an NHL-standard ice surface with seating for 8,500 spectators, a secondary practice rink, advanced player development facilities, and dedicated community programming spaces. Modern climate and energy systems ensure year-round ice quality while minimising the facility's environmental footprint.

Beyond competition, the arena will host concerts, exhibitions, graduation ceremonies, and civic events — making it a true multipurpose anchor for the region's social and economic life.

"The best arenas become landmarks. They define neighbourhoods, create jobs, and give a city something to rally around."

— Isabelle Tremblay, Urban Planning Consultant

The Arena

The Hockey Arena spans over 145,000 square feet across two levels. The main bowl seats 8,500 fans with unobstructed sightlines, premium suites, a club level, and standing-room terraces. A secondary community rink provides year-round ice access for local leagues, schools, and public programmes.

The design features a dramatic steel-and-glass facade with integrated LED panels that display team branding, event information, and community messaging. The surrounding plaza includes a fan activation zone, food court, and outdoor skating loop that extends the arena's presence into the public realm.

Sustainability is central to the design — from ammonia-free refrigeration and heat-recovery systems to solar canopy parking, rainwater collection, and energy-efficient building management that reduce operating costs by over 35%.
