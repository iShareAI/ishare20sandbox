Why FMI Stadium Matters

Modern stadiums are far more than athletic venues. They serve as economic engines, community gathering spaces, and platforms for civic engagement. FMI Stadium is designed to host professional and amateur sports, concerts, festivals, graduation ceremonies, and public events that bring the region together year-round.

The facility features retractable seating for flexible configurations, advanced accessibility throughout, sustainable energy systems, and integrated digital infrastructure that enhances the fan experience from entry to exit.

Beyond game day, FMI Stadium will anchor youth sports programming, community fitness initiatives, and educational partnerships that extend the venue's impact into neighbourhoods and schools across the region.

"The best stadiums aren't monuments to a single team — they're public assets that serve the whole community, every day of the year."

— James Ortega, Community Development Director

The Stadium

FMI Stadium spans over 280,000 square feet with a seating capacity of 25,000. The design incorporates four spectator tiers, 40 premium suites, a club level, press facilities, athlete training areas, and dedicated community spaces.

The exterior features a dynamic LED facade that can display team colours, event branding, and community messaging. The surrounding plaza includes green spaces, food courts, and a fan activation zone for pre-game experiences.

Sustainable design is embedded throughout — from rainwater harvesting and solar canopy arrays to energy-efficient lighting and climate management systems that reduce the facility's environmental footprint by over 40% compared to conventional venues.
